Who Will be Traded For the Lakers?

Michelle Jones | 04/02/2023 | 4 minute read

The trade deadline is getting closer, and the Lakers are in desperate need of help, who will stay and who will go? The Lakers have contended with top teams in both conferences despite facing injuries and fit issues with their guard heavy lineups. While they have pulled the trigger on Rui Hachimura, its clear this team needs more help, and with those first round picks still being there, who will the Lakers trade before the deadline?


The Lakers have been actively searching for help especially in terms of wing depth and shooting. With the acquisition of Rui Hachimura for Kendrick Nunn they've managed to restore some balance in their guard-heavy roster, however if they want to truly contend, that cannot be the only move they make. While the Lakers do not have much in terms of assets, they do have 5 players that teams around the league either see value in, or just have contracts large enough to bring significant return. The most obvious of which is Russell Westbrook.


JSM

Though Russ is definitely the fan favorite to be traded, especially since the resurgence of Patrick Beverley, he is not the only person that could be traded. If the Lakers were to find themselves stuck, as in teams are unwilling to even trade for Russ unless both first round picks are included regardless of the quality of return, then they might decide to make a move on the margins instead. This would mean trading one of their smaller contracts in hope of bringing a role player that could fit well with their big 3


This would save them some draft compensation as the return they would get for these players likely won't be as valuable as it would be if they traded for Russ. This could lend them some flexibility in the summer as they would then have 3 first round picks to play with.

Above you can see the stats of the Lakers' 5 most tradeable players. First we have the aforementioned Russell Westbrook whose large contract could be an avenue for the Lakers to make a splashy moves were they to include their 2 first round picks. Next we have Patrick Beverley, the Lakers' summer acquisition. While he has been playing better, he does have the most tradeable contract, and is on an expiring deal. Unfortunately he has very little trade value so he would most likely be trade filler


Now we have Lonnie Walker the IV whose in an interesting position to say the least. He is a young athletic wing who can shoot the 3, however he is on the last year of his deal, and has just come back from a knee injury. His salary is also quite small compared to that of Patrick Beverly and Westbrook's. While Walker could be an interesting addition to a deal to stack up salaries, its unclear how much other teams value him


Reaves and Christie both if traded can act as sweetener. Their very small salary makes them impossible to trade on their own, however, if the Lakers were to go for a splashy player, then these two could be included along or instead of their picks to sweeten the deal for another team due to their youth and potential as 3 and D wings.
